Migrant Shelter Crisis

Fiscal & Operational Malfeasance

The administration's handling of the migrant shelter crisis represents the most significant fiscal and operational failure of the term. The investigation reveals a systematic failure to plan, followed by an unlawful reliance on emergency powers to award lucrative contracts to political donors, and finally, a deceptive accounting strategy to hide the true costs from the public.

The "Foreseeability" Failure Timeline

The State Auditor concluded the situation was "foreseeable" and procurement was "improper and unlawful"

Data Indicated Crisis

Data clearly indicated that EA shelter demand had surpassed the system's baseline capacity of 3,600 units. The trend line was vertical and unmistakable.

The Inaction Gap

Despite this data, the Executive Office of Housing and Livable Communities (EOHLC) failed to initiate standard procurement processes for food and transportation services for eight months.

8 Months of Negligence

State of Emergency Declared

Governor Healey declares "State of Emergency." EOHLC uses this declaration to justify awarding massive no-bid contracts, claiming there was no time for a competitive process.

Emergency Ends

State of Emergency formally ended after 2 years. Over 100 communities were impacted.

The Transparency War

DiZoglio vs. Healey

Governor Healey campaigned on a platform of transparency, yet her administration has engaged in an aggressive legal and political war to prevent independent oversight by State Auditor Diana DiZoglio.

The Audit Conflict

1

The Resistance

When DiZoglio initiated the audit, the Healey administration and legislative leadership blocked access to documents, citing "Separation of Powers." Attorney General Andrea Campbell, a former subordinate of Healey, refused to support the Auditor's lawsuit to compel compliance.

2

Ballot Question 1 (2024)

In response to the obstruction, a ballot initiative was placed before voters in November 2024 explicitly authorizing the Auditor to audit the legislature. The measure passed with overwhelming bipartisan support.

3

Post-Vote Stalling

Despite the clear mandate from voters, the administration and legislative leaders have continued to drag their feet on compliance into 2025‚Äîeffectively ignoring the will of the electorate to protect their internal records.

The NDA Cover-Up

One of the key targets of DiZoglio's attempted audit is the state's use of Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs) to settle claims of sexual harassment and discrimination.
